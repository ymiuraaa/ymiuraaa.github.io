The impossible list traces its origins to its creator, Joel Runyon. It's essentially an aspirational catalog of personal challenges, goals, and experiences that push the boundaries of what an individual believes they can achieve.

The impossible list is not a bucket list, but very is similar to one. The bucket list has this stigma attached to it which is that if the creator of it does not complete it, they have "failed" themselves. I would have this dynamic long-term roadmap for myself, that changes and evolves along with me.

I will attempt to update this list regularly, in the case where I complete something, when I beat my friends in for something they're good at, or have something to add, with the later being most likely.
